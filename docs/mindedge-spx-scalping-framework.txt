================================================================================
FOUNDATION — THE TRADER'S MINDSET
================================================================================

The single most important skill a scalper can develop is thinking in probabilities. Every trade is a bet — not a certainty. A calculated bet based on the information available at that moment.

Core trading philosophy:
"You take a bet when you buy or sell an option. Probability comes into play. Before entering, ask yourself: what are the odds of this going my way? Sometimes the odds are not in your favor. The only other question is: how do I keep playing? That question is directly related to your stop-loss — capital preservation."

THE PRE-TRADE RITUAL (NEW in v2.0)
Before every entry, state these five things out loud or in writing:
1. Direction (Long or Short)
2. Entry level (specific price)
3. Stop-loss level (specific price)
4. Target level (specific price or VRVP level)
5. Confluence count (how many indicators agree)

If you cannot state all five clearly, do not enter the trade.

THREE CORE TRUTHS:
1. There are no absolutes. The market holds information you are never privy to. Accept this completely.
2. Winning and losing are too absolute. Did you execute your process correctly? A losing trade with perfect execution is a success. A winning trade taken recklessly is a failure waiting to repeat.
3. Capital preservation is everything. Your stop-loss is your right to keep playing. A trader with capital can recover. A blown account cannot.

THE SYSTEM — MULTI-TIMEFRAME FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

Three timeframes in a top-down approach. Higher timeframes define direction and key levels. Lower timeframes provide entry precision. The 30-second chart is your execution chart — never your decision chart.

Timeframe | Purpose | What You Mark
10-Minute | Bias & direction | Session high/low, Golden Pocket, overall trend — BUYS ONLY or SELLS ONLY
3-Minute | Setup identification | Fib zones as S/R, wait for price to enter Golden Pocket with room to run
30-Second | Entry execution | Fib zone as your box, candle weakening, micro-structure confirmation

SESSION TIME WINDOWS (NEW in v2.0)
Not all hours are equal. Only trade during high-velocity windows.

Window | Time (ET) | Character | Trade?
Open | 9:30–10:30am | High velocity, trend establishing, strong follow-through | YES
Mid-morning | 10:30–11:30am | Moderate momentum, fade opportunities, choppy at edges | SELECTIVE
Lunch | 11:30am–2:00pm | Low volume, random, high noise-to-signal ratio | AVOID
Power hour | 3:00–4:00pm | High velocity, institutional activity, strong closes | YES

INDICATORS — YOUR CONFIRMATION STACK
================================================================================

9 & 21 EMA — TREND & MOMENTUM
- When price is above both EMAs and 9 is above 21 (bullish stack) — longs only.
- EMA rejection on a pullback in a bullish trend = entry signal.
- NEW v2.0: If 1-minute EMA alignment contradicts 5-minute, reduce size by 50% or skip. Mixed timeframe signals are the most common cause of stopped-out trades.
- NEW v2.0: EMA slope matters as much as position. A flat 9 EMA crossing a flat 21 EMA has far less predictive value than a rising 9 crossing a rising 21.

FIBONACCI — LEVEL IDENTIFICATION
- Anchor from the most recent significant swing low to swing high (for longs).
- Golden Pocket (0.618–0.65): highest institutional interest.
- OTE Zone (0.705–0.786): optimal trade entry — deepest retracement still within bullish structure.
- 0.236 level: shallow retracement support in strong trends.
- NEW v2.0: VRVP anchored to current weekly open or session open only — not arbitrary visible range. Standardizing this eliminates the most common source of conflicting POC readings.

BOS & ChoCh — MARKET STRUCTURE
- BOS (Break of Structure): price takes out previous swing high/low — trend continuation.
- ChoCh (Change of Character): first sign of trend reversal.
- On 30-second: strong green candle breaking pullback high = micro-BOS — your trigger.
- NEW v2.0: The 1-minute chart must not contradict the 30-second trigger. A 30s micro-BOS during a 1-minute bearish sequence is a false signal. Always confirm up one timeframe.

VRVP & SVP — VOLUME FOOTPRINT
- POC (Point of Control): highest-volume price level — a magnet for price.
- Value Area High/Low (VAH/VAL): range where 70% of volume traded.
- Enter inside the value area for high-probability trades.
- NEW v2.0: Anchor VRVP to weekly open or session open only — not visible range.
- NEW v2.0: Price outside the value area returns to it 80% of the time. When entering outside VA, your target IS the POC, not a Fibonacci level.

GEX LEVELS — GAMMA EXPOSURE (MANDATORY)
- GEX is no longer optional for SPX scalping. Mark it before the session opens.
- Call walls: resistance. Put walls: support. HVL: the flip level.
- Above HVL: market makers stabilize — buy the dip behavior dominates.
- Below HVL: market makers amplify — sell the rip behavior dominates.
- NEW v2.0: Never enter a trade if your target is blocked by a major gamma wall. The gamma wall IS your target, not an obstacle to pass through.
- Source: SpotGamma, Squeeze Metrics, or similar. Check daily at 9:00am ET.

FVGs & LIQUIDITY — CONFIRMATION & TARGETS
- Fair Value Gap: gap between high of candle 1 and low of candle 3 in a three-candle sequence.
- Price returns to fill FVGs before continuing — use as entry zones and targets.
- Liquidity clusters above recent highs and below recent lows — smart money drives price there first.
- A sweep of liquidity followed by immediate reversal is often the real trade.

================================================================================
EXECUTION — STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS
================================================================================

STEP 01 — PRE-SESSION PREPARATION (9:00am ET)
- Check GEX levels from SpotGamma or Squeeze Metrics before every session.
- Mark session high and low from yesterday on the 10-minute chart.
- Identify bias: buys only or sells only today based on 10m structure.
- Mark Golden Pocket and key Fibonacci zones.
- Note HVL level: are we above or below it?
- Confirm you are trading during an approved session window (9:30–10:30am or 3:00–4:00pm ET).

STEP 02 — IDENTIFY THE SETUP (3-Minute or 5-Minute)
- Wait for price to enter your pre-marked Fib zone (Golden Pocket or OTE).
- Confirm EMA stack aligns with directional bias.
- Look for BOS or ChoCh confirming structure.
- Check VRVP — anchored to session open. Are you inside the value area?
- Verify GEX — is your target blocked by a gamma wall? If so, do not enter.
- NEW v2.0: If price has not triggered within 3 candles on the execution timeframe, the setup is INVALIDATED.

STEP 03 — EXECUTE ON THE 30-SECOND CHART
- Drop to 30-second. The Fib zone is your entry box.
- Wait for the pullback candle (red in a long setup).
- Trigger: strong candle breaking the high of the pullback candle (micro-BOS).
- NEW v2.0: Confirm the 1-minute chart does NOT contradict the 30s trigger before entering.
- Enter at or just after candle close. Stop-loss: just below the Fib zone boundary.
- State your five pre-trade checks out loud before clicking.

STEP 04 — TRADE MANAGEMENT — POINT-BASED RULES (NEW v2.0)
- Move stop to breakeven after +8 points (not "20%" — be specific).
- Take 50% off at +12 points. Let the remaining 50% run.
- Trail the runner: move stop up by 4 points for every 4 points of further gain.
- Exit criteria (specific, not subjective): EMA9 rolling over on 30s chart, OR bearish engulfing candle on 1-minute, OR price closing below 9 EMA on execution timeframe.
- Never add to a losing position.

STEP 05 — RE-ENTRY RULE (NEW v2.0)
- If stopped out at breakeven and original thesis is still valid: ONE re-entry permitted.
- Re-entry conditions: price must return to original Fib zone AND a fresh 30s trigger must form.
- Use original stop parameters. No widening for the re-entry.
- After two stopped-out attempts at the same level, the thesis is invalid. Move on.

STEP 06 — POST-TRADE REVIEW
- Log every trade: entry, exit, what you saw, how you felt, confluence count.
- Rate setup quality — not the outcome. A losing trade with perfect execution is a success.
- Ask: was my entry in the OTE zone? Was there EMA confluence? Was GEX checked? Was I inside the value area?
- One thing done well, one thing to improve. That is the complete review.

NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES — v2.0
================================================================================

Rule 1: Only trade during approved session windows — 9:30–10:30am or 3:00–4:00pm ET. A valid setup in the lunch hour is not a valid trade.
Rule 2: Only trade in the direction of the 10-minute bias. Fighting the dominant trend is the most common reason scalps fail.
Rule 3: Check GEX before every session. No exceptions. If you have not checked it, you are not ready to trade.
Rule 4: Never enter without at least 3 confluences. One indicator is a signal. Three are a setup. Less than three is gambling.
Rule 5: If 1m EMA contradicts 5m, reduce size 50% or skip. Mixed timeframe EMA signals are the number one cause of stopped-out valid setups.
Rule 6: Setup expires after 3 candles on the execution timeframe. A setup that has not triggered in 3 candles is no longer the same setup. Re-evaluate from the top down.
Rule 7: Stop-loss set before entry. Always. Discretionary stops get moved. Pre-set stops protect capital unconditionally.
Rule 8: Move stop to breakeven at +8 points. Specific, not percentage-based.
Rule 9: Take 50% off at +12 points. Lock in real profit. Let the runner work with house money.
Rule 10: One re-entry permitted if stopped at breakeven. Price must return to Fib zone and form a fresh trigger. Original stop parameters apply.
Rule 11: Never average down on a losing position. Adding to a loser turns a bad trade into a catastrophic one.
Rule 12: Do not trade within 30 minutes of a major news event. GEX levels and technical structure become irrelevant around scheduled catalysts.
Rule 13: Maximum 3 losing trades in a session — then stop. After 3 losses, your judgment is compromised. Step away. Come back tomorrow.
Rule 14: Log every trade. No exceptions. Unlogged trades are unreviewed mistakes that will repeat indefinitely.
